Reading the numbers

  • Throughput: ⚪ parity on Totals (−0.8%) and per-command (HEXPIRE / HPERSIST / FT.SEARCH all within ±2%). Master CV 2–3%, Joan CV 3–6% — delta well inside noise.
  • HPERSIST tail story changed with more datapoints: at n=3 the v1 report saw a "clear improvement" (p99 −32.5%, p99.9 −21.6%). At n=5 those collapse to ⚪ p99 +1.4% and ⚪ p99.9 +5.2% — back inside noise. The earlier "improvement" was 1–2 specific master runs being unusually slow on HPERSIST tail, not a real PR effect.
  • FT.SEARCH tail regression reproduces and gets more credible: 🔴 p99 +44.0% / p99.9 +39.0% with master CV ~9% and Joan CV ~30–35%. Both sides' envelopes overlap but Joan's mean is ~45 s above master's on p99 — a real signal, not n=3 noise as the v1 report hedged.
  • HEXPIRE tail mild regression: 🔴 p99 +7.1% / p99.9 +16.4%. Smaller than FT.SEARCH but consistently in the same direction across the 5 Joan runs (CV 14–32%).
  • Avg latency mixed: ⚪ Totals −3.8% (slight overall improvement) but 🔴 HEXPIRE / HPERSIST avg both ~+17% — writes spend more time blocked client-side on Joan, even though server-side per-command latency on those is microseconds (memtier closed-loop scheduling: write commands wait behind the slow FT.SEARCH ahead of them in the sequence; if FT.SEARCH gets longer tails on Joan, every write behind it pays).
  • Net assessment vs v1 report: 🔴 FT.SEARCH tail regression on Joan is real and reproduces at n=5; HPERSIST tail improvement was n=3 noise and is gone now. Throughput parity holds. Recommend Joan investigates what in the HFE fast-path refactor adds tail variance to the FT.SEARCH read path.
