Reading the numbers

  • Throughput: Joan delivers small wins across all four streams (~+2% to +3%), but at this magnitude on cluster-04-threads-6 the deltas sit close to the noise floor (master CV 2.0–6.1%, Joan CV 3.0–11.4%).
  • Tail latencies are mixed and not in Joan's favor on FT.SEARCH / HEXPIRE. Joan's runs show much higher tail variance (Joan p99 CV up to 45% vs master's ≤10%), and the mean p99 / p99.9 are 20–72% above master for FT.SEARCH and HEXPIRE. This is driven by 1 of 3 Joan datapoints having a very long tail — see raw values below. The signal isn't statistically clean at n=3.
  • HPERSIST tail latency clearly improves on Joan: p99 −32.5%, p99.9 −21.6%, both larger than the CV envelope.
  • Net assessment: throughput-neutral with possible HPERSIST tail wins; FT.SEARCH/HEXPIRE tail story is inconclusive at n=3 (high Joan CV — needs more datapoints to resolve whether it's a real regression or one bad run).
